# Blood Bank Management System

## Problem Statement

Blood banks are responsible for collecting, storing, and distributing blood to hospitals and patients. Traditional manual methods of managing donor information, blood inventory, and blood requests often lead to errors, delays, data redundancy, and difficulties in tracking blood availability. A computerized Blood Bank Management System is required to automate these activities, improve accuracy, and ensure timely blood supply.

## Main Objective

To develop a Blood Bank Management System that automates donor management, blood inventory management, and blood distribution processes efficiently and securely.

## Specific Objectives

* To maintain donor registration and records.
* To manage blood collection and storage details.
* To monitor blood stock availability based on blood groups.
* To process blood requests from hospitals and patients.
* To track blood issuance and distribution.
* To generate reports related to blood bank activities.
* To reduce manual paperwork and improve operational efficiency.

## Proposed Solution

The proposed Blood Bank Management System is a database-driven application that manages donor information, blood donation records, blood inventory, and blood requests in a centralized system. The application enables administrators to update blood stock, process requests, issue blood units, and generate reports. The system improves efficiency, reduces errors, and ensures accurate record management.

## System Workflow

* User logs into the system.
* Donor information is registered and stored.
* Blood donation details are entered into the database.
* Blood testing and verification are completed.
* Blood inventory is updated according to blood group.
* Blood requests are received from hospitals or patients.
* The system checks blood availability.
* Available blood units are issued.
* Inventory records are updated automatically.
* Reports are generated for management purposes.
* User logs out of the system.
